# Meeting Preparation Workflow

## Step 1: Meeting Context
- Check working memory for client relationship details, recent issues, and prior meeting notes
- Identify meeting type: kickoff, weekly check-in, monthly review, quarterly business review (QBR), ad-hoc issue resolution
- Confirm attendees and their roles (who needs what from this meeting?)
- Review previous meeting action items — what's complete, what's outstanding?

## Step 2: Agenda Creation
Build a structured agenda with time allocations:

**Weekly Check-in (30 min):**
1. Quick wins / highlights (5 min)
2. Performance snapshot (5 min)
3. Project status updates (10 min)
4. Blockers & items needing client input (5 min)
5. Action items & next steps (5 min)

**Monthly Review (45-60 min):**
1. Month highlights & wins (5 min)
2. KPI review with MoM trends (15 min)
3. Channel deep-dive on focus area (10 min)
4. Strategic discussion / client questions (10 min)
5. Next month priorities & action items (10 min)

**QBR (60-90 min):**
1. Quarter performance summary (15 min)
2. Goal progress review (10 min)
3. Strategic wins and learnings (10 min)
4. Competitive landscape update (10 min)
5. Next quarter strategy & roadmap (20 min)
6. Budget & resource discussion (10 min)
7. Open discussion (10 min)

## Step 3: Talking Points & Data
- Pull latest metrics for key KPIs
- Prepare answers for likely client questions ("Why did X drop?", "What about competitor Y?")
- Identify 1-2 proactive strategic recommendations to present
- Prepare any visuals or screen-share materials

## Step 4: Pre-Meeting Delivery
- Share agenda with client 24 hours before the meeting
- Include any pre-read materials (reports, proposals)
- Confirm meeting time and attendees

## Step 5: Post-Meeting Template
Prepare the follow-up recap structure:
- Key decisions made
- Action items with owners and deadlines
- Next meeting date
- Any scope or strategy changes documented

## Quality Checks
- [ ] Agenda is realistic for the meeting duration
- [ ] Action items from last meeting are accounted for
- [ ] Data is current and accurate
- [ ] Proactive recommendations are prepared (not just reactive reporting)
- [ ] Potential difficult questions have prepared responses
- Save prep notes in working memory for reference during and after the call
