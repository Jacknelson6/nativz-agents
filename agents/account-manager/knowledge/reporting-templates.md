# Reporting Templates — Expert Knowledge Base

## Weekly Status Report Structure

### Format: Quick, Scannable, Action-Oriented
Sent every Monday by 12pm. Clients should be able to read this in 2 minutes.

```
## [Client Name] — Weekly Update (Week of [Date])

### 🟢 Performance Snapshot
| Metric | This Week | Last Week | Change | Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Spend | $X | $X | +X% | $X |
| Revenue/Leads | $X / #X | $X / #X | +X% | $X |
| ROAS / CPL | X.Xx / $X | X.Xx / $X | +X% | X.Xx / $X |
| [Primary KPI] | X | X | +X% | X |

### 🏆 Wins This Week
- [Specific win with numbers]
- [Specific win with numbers]

### ⚠️ Challenges / Watch Items
- [Issue and what we're doing about it]

### 🎯 Actions Taken
- [What we changed/launched/tested]

### 📋 Plan for Next Week
- [What we'll focus on]

### 🔒 Blockers / Needs from Client
- [Anything we need from them, with deadline]
```

### Rules
- **Traffic light system**: 🟢 On track / 🟡 Attention needed / 🔴 Off track — put one at the top so they immediately know the status
- **Numbers, not narratives** — save storytelling for the monthly report
- **Compare to target** — week-over-week is noise; performance vs target is what matters
- **No jargon** — "we decreased cost per lead by 15%" not "we optimized tCPA bids in the auction layer"

## Monthly Performance Report Structure

### Format: Comprehensive, Strategic, Storytelling
Delivered within 5 business days of month end. Presented live on a call, then sent as a document/deck.

### Section 1: Executive Summary (1 Slide/Paragraph)
3-5 sentences covering:
- Overall performance vs goals (hit/miss/exceeded)
- The biggest win of the month
- The biggest challenge and what you did about it
- One forward-looking insight or opportunity

> "February was a strong month — we exceeded revenue targets by 12% while maintaining ROAS above 4×. Our top-performing creative was the UGC testimonial series, which drove 35% of total conversions. CPMs increased 18% due to Valentine's Day competition, which we offset by shifting budget to Google Shopping. In March, we're launching a new product line campaign and expect continued growth."

### Section 2: KPI Dashboard
| Metric | February | January | MoM Change | Target | vs Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Total Spend | $15,000 | $14,200 | +5.6% | $15,000 | ✅ On budget |
| Revenue | $67,500 | $56,800 | +18.8% | $60,000 | ✅ +12.5% |
| ROAS | 4.5× | 4.0× | +12.5% | 4.0× | ✅ Above target |
| CPA | $22.50 | $26.10 | -13.8% | $25.00 | ✅ Below target |
| Orders/Leads | 667 | 544 | +22.6% | 600 | ✅ +11.2% |
| AOV | $101.20 | $104.41 | -3.1% | $100.00 | ✅ Above target |
| CTR | 1.8% | 1.5% | +20% | 1.5% | ✅ |
| CVR | 3.2% | 2.8% | +14.3% | 3.0% | ✅ |

Include a **trend chart** showing the last 3-6 months. Context > snapshots.

### Section 3: Channel Breakdown
For each active platform (Meta, Google, TikTok, etc.):
- Spend and % of total
- Key metrics (ROAS, CPA, CTR)
- Top-performing campaigns
- What was tested and results
- Recommendations for next month

### Section 4: Creative Performance
- **Top 3 creatives** by primary metric (with thumbnails/screenshots)
- **Why they worked** — identify the pattern (hook type, format, creator, message)
- **Worst 3 creatives** and what we learned
- **Creative fatigue report** — which creatives need refreshing
- **Next month's creative plan** — concepts to test, based on learnings

### Section 5: Audience Insights
- Which audiences converted best (with CPA/ROAS by audience)
- Any new audience discoveries
- Audience saturation signals (frequency, rising CPMs)
- Recommendations for audience expansion

### Section 6: Competitive Landscape (Optional, Quarterly is Fine)
- Notable competitor ad activity (use Meta Ad Library, TikTok Creative Center)
- Any market trends affecting performance
- Opportunities based on competitive gaps

### Section 7: Next Month Plan
- **Budget allocation** — any changes and why
- **Testing plan** — what we'll test (creative, audience, budget)
- **Calendar** — key dates (holidays, events, sales) affecting strategy
- **Goals** — specific KPI targets for next month

## Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Framework

### Purpose
Step back from tactical performance to evaluate strategic progress. This is where you demonstrate long-term value and align on future direction.

### QBR Agenda (60-90 Minutes)

**1. Quarter in Review (20 min)**
- Performance vs quarterly goals (table with all KPIs)
- Quarter-over-quarter and year-over-year trends
- Total ROI delivered (revenue generated vs total investment including agency fees)
- Key wins and milestones

**2. Strategic Analysis (20 min)**
- What worked and why (creative themes, audience insights, platform mix)
- What didn't work and what we learned
- Market/competitive landscape changes
- Platform updates that affected performance (algorithm changes, new features)

**3. Customer/Audience Insights (10 min)**
- Who is converting (demographics, behaviors, geographic)
- Changes in customer profile over time
- Insights from post-purchase surveys or customer feedback
- Content themes that resonated most

**4. Next Quarter Strategy (20 min)**
- Updated goals and KPI targets
- Budget recommendations (increase, decrease, reallocate)
- New initiatives or tests planned
- Content/creative strategy evolution
- Platform expansion or consolidation recommendations

**5. Scope & Relationship Review (10 min)**
- Is the current scope still aligned with goals?
- Any additional services that would drive value?
- Feedback on communication, process, and deliverables
- Any concerns or requests

### QBR Best Practices
- **Send the deck 24 hours before** so the client can review and come with questions
- **Start with their goals, not your metrics** — frame everything in terms of their business objectives
- **Show cumulative value** — "Over the last quarter, we generated $X in revenue on $Y in spend, delivering Z× return on your total investment"
- **Be honest about misses** — clients respect transparency more than spin
- **End with a clear plan** — they should leave knowing exactly what happens next

## How to Tell the Story Behind the Numbers

### The Narrative Framework
Don't just present data — explain **what happened, why it happened, and what we're doing about it.**

**For wins:**
> "Revenue increased 18% this month. This was driven by our new UGC creative series, which achieved 2× the ROAS of our previous best-performing ads. The winning formula was creator testimonials with a problem-solution-result structure, filmed in a native phone-quality style. We're doubling down on this format next month with 5 new creators."

**For misses:**
> "CPA increased 15% this month, above our target of $25. The primary driver was CPM inflation during the Valentine's Day period (+22% industry-wide). Our response was to shift 20% of Meta budget to Google Shopping, which maintained a $18 CPA. With seasonal pressure behind us, we expect CPA to normalize by mid-March."

### Storytelling Rules
1. **Lead with the insight, not the data** — "Our audience is shifting younger" not "18-24 demo increased from 22% to 31%"
2. **Explain the 'so what'** — every data point should connect to a business implication
3. **Compare to something meaningful** — vs target, vs industry benchmark, vs last year (not just last month)
4. **Use visuals** — charts and graphs tell stories better than tables for trend data
5. **Highlight the unexpected** — clients remember surprises, not confirmations

## Benchmarking & Goal Setting

### Setting Goals
**SMART framework for paid media goals:**
- **Specific**: "Achieve 4× blended ROAS on Meta" (not "improve ROAS")
- **Measurable**: Tied to a specific metric with a specific number
- **Achievable**: Based on current performance + reasonable improvement (10-20% improvement per quarter is aggressive but achievable)
- **Relevant**: Tied to business outcomes (revenue, leads, growth)
- **Time-bound**: Monthly and quarterly targets

### Goal-Setting Process
1. **Baseline**: What's the average performance over the last 90 days?
2. **Trajectory**: Is performance improving, declining, or flat?
3. **Benchmarks**: How does this compare to industry averages?
4. **Ambition**: What does the client need/want to achieve?
5. **Reality check**: Is the budget sufficient for the target? (Use: Target Revenue = Budget × Target ROAS)
6. **Set the goal**: Stretch but achievable — hitting goals builds trust, chronic misses erode it
