# Client Communication Guide

## Meeting Templates

### Kickoff Meeting Agenda (60-90 min)
```
1. Introductions & Roles (10 min)
   - Agency team members and responsibilities
   - Client team members and decision-making authority
   
2. Business Overview (15 min)
   - Company background, products/services, target audience
   - Business goals for the next 6-12 months
   - Key competitors
   
3. Current Marketing Overview (15 min)
   - What's been done before (agencies, in-house, tools)
   - What's working, what's not
   - Existing assets, accounts, access
   
4. Scope & Deliverables Review (15 min)
   - Walk through the SOW/contract
   - Confirm deliverables, timelines, and milestones
   - Clarify any ambiguous items
   
5. KPIs & Success Metrics (10 min)
   - Agree on primary and secondary KPIs
   - Set realistic targets with timelines
   - Define reporting cadence and format
   
6. Communication Preferences (10 min)
   - Preferred communication channel (email, Slack, Teams, etc.)
   - Update frequency (weekly email, bi-weekly call, etc.)
   - Response time expectations (both sides)
   - Escalation contacts
   
7. Access & Onboarding Needs (10 min)
   - Platform access list (ad accounts, analytics, CMS, social accounts)
   - Brand guidelines, style guides, asset libraries
   - Technical contacts for dev requests
   
8. Next Steps & Action Items (5 min)
   - Immediate action items with owners and deadlines
   - First deliverable timeline
   - Next meeting date
```

### Weekly Check-in Template (30 min)
```
Subject: [Client] Weekly Sync — [Date]

Agenda:
1. Wins & highlights since last week (5 min)
2. Performance snapshot — key metrics (5 min)
3. Project status updates (10 min)
   - [Project A]: Status, blockers, ETA
   - [Project B]: Status, blockers, ETA
4. Items needing client input/approval (5 min)
5. Action items & next steps (5 min)

Pre-read: [Link to dashboard or brief update]
```

### Monthly Review Template (45-60 min)
```
Subject: [Client] Monthly Review — [Month Year]

Agenda:
1. Month highlights & wins (5 min)
2. KPI dashboard review (10 min)
   - Primary KPIs: actual vs target
   - MoM and YoY trends
3. Channel deep-dives (15 min)
   - [Channel 1]: Performance, activities, insights
   - [Channel 2]: Performance, activities, insights
4. Strategic discussion (10 min)
   - [Topic for client input]
   - Upcoming opportunities or concerns
5. Next month plan & priorities (5 min)
6. Open Q&A (5 min)

Attached: [Month] Performance Report
```

### Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Template (90 min)
```
Subject: [Client] Q[X] Business Review — [Year]

Agenda:
1. Quarter performance summary (15 min)
   - Revenue/lead impact from marketing
   - KPIs: actual vs quarterly targets
   - Key wins and milestones
   
2. Channel performance analysis (20 min)
   - Channel-by-channel breakdown
   - ROI by channel
   - What worked, what didn't, what we learned
   
3. Competitive landscape (10 min)
   - Competitor movements and market shifts
   - Industry benchmarks comparison
   
4. Strategic review (15 min)
   - Are we on track for annual goals?
   - What needs to change?
   - New opportunities identified
   
5. Next quarter roadmap (20 min)
   - Strategic priorities ranked by expected impact
   - Resource requirements
   - Budget discussion
   - Key milestones and deliverables
   
6. Open discussion (10 min)
   - Client feedback on partnership
   - Upcoming business changes to factor in
   - Team or process adjustments
```

## Escalation Procedures

### Escalation Levels

**Level 1: Standard Issues (Handle immediately)**
- Minor delays (1-2 days)
- Small scope clarifications
- Routine platform issues
- **Action:** Notify client proactively, provide updated timeline
- **Owner:** Account coordinator / project manager

**Level 2: Significant Issues (Escalate within 4 hours)**
- Missed deadlines
- Campaign underperformance (>20% below target)
- Budget pacing issues (over/under by >15%)
- Client dissatisfaction expressed
- **Action:** Immediate notification to client with: what happened, why, action plan, timeline
- **Owner:** Account manager + department lead
- **Template:**
```
Hi [Name],

I want to flag an issue proactively: [brief description].

What happened: [factual explanation]
Why: [root cause]
Our action plan: [specific steps]
Timeline: [when it will be resolved]

I take responsibility for this and am personally overseeing the resolution. 
I'll update you by [date/time]. Happy to jump on a call if helpful.

[Name]
```

**Level 3: Critical Issues (Escalate immediately)**
- Account suspension or policy violation
- Data breach or security concern
- Major campaign failure or overspend
- Client threatening to leave / contract dispute
- Legal or compliance issues
- **Action:** Immediate phone call to client + internal leadership notification
- **Owner:** Account director + agency leadership
- **Internal escalation:** Notify via Slack #critical-issues channel, pull in relevant department heads

**Level 4: Crisis (All hands)**
- Public PR issue involving the client
- Platform-wide outage affecting multiple clients
- Legal action or regulatory investigation
- **Action:** Crisis team assembled, unified communication plan, client called within 30 minutes
- **Owner:** Agency leadership + legal if applicable

### Escalation Communication Rules
1. **Never hide problems.** Bad news delivered early is manageable; bad news delivered late is a crisis.
2. **Lead with solutions.** Don't just report the problem — present your action plan.
3. **Own mistakes.** If it's our fault, say so clearly and without excuses.
4. **Document everything.** Every escalation gets a written record: what happened, when, actions taken, resolution.
5. **Follow up.** After resolution, send a summary and any process changes to prevent recurrence.
6. **Post-mortem.** For Level 3+ incidents, conduct an internal post-mortem within 1 week.

### Client Health Scoring
Monitor these signals for early warning:
- **🟢 Healthy:** Responsive to emails, positive meeting tone, approving work on time, renewing/expanding
- **🟡 At Risk:** Slower responses, questioning value, pushing back on reports, missed meetings
- **🔴 Churn Risk:** Unresponsive, escalating complaints, requesting "pause" or reduced scope, comparing to competitors

**At Risk Response Protocol:**
1. Schedule a candid conversation (not during regular status call)
2. Listen first — understand their concerns fully before responding
3. Present a concrete action plan with measurable milestones
4. Offer a value-add (bonus audit, strategy session, etc.)
5. Increase communication frequency temporarily
6. Involve agency leadership if needed
7. Document the plan and review progress in 2-4 weeks

## Communication Style Guide

### Tone by Client Type
- **Startup/SMB:** Casual, proactive, explanatory (they may not know marketing jargon)
- **Mid-Market:** Professional but warm, data-driven, strategic recommendations
- **Enterprise:** Formal, thorough, process-oriented, executive summaries required
- **Creator/Personal Brand:** Collaborative, creative, trend-focused, platform-native language

### Email Response Times
- **Standard:** Within 4 business hours
- **Urgent:** Within 1 hour
- **Weekend/After Hours:** Acknowledge within 12 hours, full response next business day (unless critical)

### Communication Don'ts
- Don't use jargon without explanation (not every client knows what "CBO" or "canonical" means)
- Don't send reports without context ("Here's the report" → "Here's the report — key takeaway is X")
- Don't over-promise timelines (add 20% buffer to all estimates)
- Don't CC the whole team on client emails (one point of contact)
- Don't discuss internal team issues with clients
- Don't respond emotionally to criticism — listen, acknowledge, solve
