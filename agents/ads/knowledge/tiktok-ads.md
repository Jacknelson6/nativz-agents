# TikTok Ads — Expert Knowledge Base

## Ad Formats

### Spark Ads
Spark Ads boost existing organic TikTok posts (your own or a creator's) as paid ads. This is the **#1 format for most advertisers** in 2025.

Why Spark Ads win:
- **Native feel** — appears as a regular TikTok post, not an ad. Users engage more.
- **Social proof accumulates** — likes, comments, shares on the ad persist on the organic post
- **Higher CTR** than standard in-feed ads (1.5-3× in most tests)
- **Comment section** becomes a sales tool — pin positive comments, respond to objections

How to run Spark Ads:
1. Creator posts organic TikTok (or you post from your brand account)
2. Creator generates an **authorization code** (Settings → Creator tools → TikTok Ads authorization)
3. You input the code in TikTok Ads Manager → create ad → select "Use TikTok post"
4. Authorization codes last 7, 30, or 365 days — always request 365

Best practices:
- Let the organic post get **initial traction** (24-48 hours) before boosting — the algorithm gives organic posts a test audience; if it performs well organically, it'll perform even better as a Spark Ad
- Don't edit the post after running as a Spark Ad — resets performance data
- Use the creator's account for the ad identity, not your brand account (higher trust signals)

### In-Feed Ads
Standard video ads that appear in the For You feed. You upload the creative directly to Ads Manager.

When to use over Spark Ads:
- You want to **A/B test** hooks, CTAs, or copy without affecting organic posts
- You need to use **TikTok's interactive add-ons** (polls, countdowns, display cards)
- You're running **dynamic creative optimization** (DCO) — TikTok assembles combinations of hooks, bodies, CTAs
- You don't have creator content to boost

Specs:
- **Aspect ratio**: 9:16 (mandatory — 16:9 and 1:1 technically work but perform terribly)
- **Resolution**: 1080×1920 minimum
- **Length**: 5-60 seconds (sweet spot: **15-30 seconds**)
- **File size**: Up to 500MB
- **Captions**: Auto-generated or upload SRT file

### TopView
Premium placement — first ad a user sees when opening TikTok. Full-screen, up to 60 seconds, auto-plays with sound.

- **CPMs**: $50-100+ (varies by market and season)
- **Use when**: Major product launches, brand moments, reaching mass audiences quickly
- **Minimum spend**: Typically $50K+ per day (reservation-based, not auction)
- **Best for**: CPG brands, entertainment, apps with broad appeal
- Not viable for most SMBs or agencies under $500K/month total spend

### Branded Effects / Hashtag Challenges
- Branded effects: Custom AR filters/stickers (minimum $100K+)
- Hashtag challenges: Brand-sponsored challenge (minimum $150K+, often $500K+)
- **Skip these unless** you're a major brand with massive budget. The ROI is hard to measure and the minimums are prohibitive.

## Creative Best Practices

### The 2-Second Rule
TikTok's average watch time before scroll is **1.5-2 seconds**. Your hook must land immediately.

**Hook types that stop the scroll:**
1. **Text hook on screen** — Bold text overlay that teases the payoff: "The $12 product that replaced my $200 serum"
2. **Visual disruption** — Unexpected imagery, fast motion, hand reaching toward camera
3. **Sound hook** — Trending audio, loud/unexpected sound, ASMR texture
4. **Direct address** — "POV: you just found the best [product] on TikTok"
5. **Controversy/hot take** — "Unpopular opinion: [bold claim about your category]"
6. **Before/after tease** — Show the result first, then "here's how"

### Native Feel is Non-Negotiable
TikTok users scroll past anything that looks like an ad. Your content must feel organic.

Rules:
- **Film on a phone** (or make it look phone-filmed). No studio lighting, no 4K DSLR look.
- **Use trending sounds** — even as background music, trending audio boosts distribution
- **Creator > brand spokesperson** — UGC from relatable creators outperforms brand-produced content 3-5× on average
- **Fast pacing** — cut every 2-3 seconds minimum. Static shots kill retention.
- **Text overlays** — 80% of TikTok is watched with captions/text visible; always add text
- **No logos in first 3 seconds** — immediate branding screams "ad" and triggers scroll
- **Aspect ratio must be 9:16** — any letterboxing tanks performance

### Content Frameworks That Work
**Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS)**
"Are you tired of [problem]? I tried everything and nothing worked. Then I found [product]. [Show result]."
→ 15-20 seconds, direct-to-camera, works for beauty/health/productivity

**Unboxing / First Impressions**
"Let's see if [product] lives up to the hype." → Genuine reaction, ASMR opening, close-up textures
→ 20-30 seconds, works for physical products

**Day-in-my-life Integration**
Product appears naturally within a lifestyle vlog. No hard sell.
→ 30-60 seconds, works for food/beverage, fashion, tech

**Tutorial / How-To**
"3 ways to use [product] you didn't know about"
→ 15-30 seconds, works for multi-use products, software

**Stitch/Duet Style**
React to a trending video or customer testimonial. Builds on existing engagement patterns.
→ 15-25 seconds

### Creative Volume & Testing
- Launch **3-5 new creatives per week** for active campaigns
- Each creative should test **one variable**: hook, CTA, creator, format, or angle
- Kill creatives when **CTR drops below 0.8%** or **CPM rises 30%+** from baseline
- Top-performing creatives last **7-21 days** on average before fatigue
- **Iterate winners**: Take your best hook + change the body. Take your best body + change the hook.

## Targeting

### Interest Targeting
TikTok offers interest categories (similar to Meta). Useful for:
- New accounts with no pixel data
- Narrowing down for niche products
- Combining with behavioral targeting for precision

**Tip**: TikTok's interest targeting is less sophisticated than Meta's. Keep audiences **broad** (5M+) and let the algorithm find converters.

### Behavioral Targeting
Target based on what users do on TikTok:
- **Video interactions**: Liked, commented, shared videos in a category
- **Creator interactions**: Followed specific types of creators
- **Hashtag interactions**: Engaged with specific hashtags

Behavioral targeting is **TikTok's unique advantage** — it's based on actual in-app behavior, not inferred interests.

### Custom Audiences
- **Customer file**: Upload email/phone lists (match rates: 30-50%, lower than Meta)
- **Website traffic**: Pixel-based retargeting (requires pixel setup)
- **App activity**: For app advertisers
- **Engagement**: People who interacted with your TikTok content (viewed, liked, followed)
- **Lead generation**: People who opened/submitted your lead gen forms

### Lookalike Audiences
- Based on custom audiences, similar to Meta's LALs
- Audience expansion options: Narrow, Balanced, Broad
- **Start with Balanced**, expand to Broad for scaling
- Source audience: Use purchasers or high-intent converters (not page viewers)
- Minimum source size: 1,000 (10,000+ recommended)

### Smart Targeting & Automated Targeting
TikTok's algorithmic targeting (similar to Meta's broad/Advantage+):
- Works best with **strong creative** that self-selects the audience
- Requires **50+ conversions per week** per ad group to optimize effectively
- **Recommended for scaling** once you've identified winning creatives through targeted testing

## TikTok Pixel & Events API

### Pixel Setup
Install TikTok Pixel on your website for tracking and optimization.

**Standard events** (configure in priority order):
1. `CompletePayment` (equivalent to Meta's Purchase)
2. `PlaceAnOrder` (InitiateCheckout)
3. `AddToCart`
4. `ViewContent`
5. `ClickButton` (for lead gen CTAs)
6. `SubmitForm` (for lead gen)

**Setup options:**
- **Shopify/WooCommerce integration**: One-click setup, recommended for ecommerce
- **Google Tag Manager**: Custom template available, more control
- **Manual pixel code**: For custom sites

### Events API (Server-Side)
TikTok's server-side tracking (equivalent to Meta's CAPI). **Required in 2025** for accurate attribution.

- Send events from your server directly to TikTok
- Bypasses ad blockers and iOS restrictions
- **Deduplication**: Use the same `event_id` for pixel and Events API events
- Setup: Direct API integration, Shopify native, or partners (e.g., Stape, Elevar)

**Advanced Matching Parameters** (send with events for better match rates):
- Email (SHA256 hashed)
- Phone number (SHA256 hashed)
- External ID
- IP address
- User agent
- TikTok click ID (ttclid)

### Attribution Settings
- Default: **7-day click, 1-day view** (same as Meta)
- TikTok's self-reported attribution is typically **over-reported by 20-50%**
- Cross-reference with Shopify/GA4/post-purchase surveys
- Use TikTok's **Attribution Analytics** tool to compare windows

## Budget & Scaling

### Budget Minimums
- **Campaign minimum**: $50/day (CBO)
- **Ad group minimum**: $20/day (ABO)
- **Practical minimum for optimization**: $50-100/day per ad group (need enough data for the algorithm)
- **Recommended starting budget**: $100-150/day per ad group to exit learning phase in 3-5 days

### Learning Phase
- TikTok ad groups enter a **learning phase** when created or significantly edited
- Need **~50 conversions** to exit learning phase
- During learning, performance fluctuates — don't judge or make changes
- Budget changes >30% or targeting changes reset learning phase

### Scaling Strategies

**Vertical Scaling (Budget Increases)**
- Increase budget by **20-30% every 2-3 days** (TikTok is more forgiving than Meta on budget jumps)
- If CPA spikes >40% for 2+ days, revert to previous budget
- Some advertisers successfully do **50% budget increases** on TikTok (test carefully)

**Horizontal Scaling (New Ad Groups/Campaigns)**
- Duplicate winning ad groups with different targeting or new creatives
- Launch new campaigns to test different optimization events (e.g., switch from AddToCart to CompletePayment optimization)
- Test different bid strategies (cost cap vs maximum delivery)

**Creative Scaling**
- The most important lever. TikTok creative fatigues **faster** than Meta.
- Plan for **5-10 new creatives per week** at scale ($5K+/day)
- Build a **creator pipeline**: 5-10 UGC creators on retainer, each producing 2-4 videos/month
- Repurpose: Take winning organic TikToks (yours or creators') and run as Spark Ads

### Bid Strategies
**Maximum Delivery (default)**
- Spends full budget to maximize conversions
- **Use for**: Testing, learning phase, when volume > efficiency

**Cost Cap**
- Set target CPA; TikTok tries to stay at or below
- **Use for**: Scaling with CPA guardrails
- Set at **actual target CPA** (not lower) — TikTok will underspend aggressively if too tight
- Expect 10-30% underspend vs budget

**Minimum ROAS**
- Set ROAS floor for purchase-optimized campaigns
- **Use for**: Ecommerce scaling
- Set at **70-80% of actual ROAS** to give algorithm room

### TikTok vs Meta: Key Differences for Media Buyers
| Factor | TikTok | Meta |
|---|---|---|
| Creative lifespan | 7-14 days | 14-30 days |
| Creative volume needed | Higher (2×) | Lower |
| Audience targeting precision | Lower | Higher |
| CPMs (US) | $8-15 | $10-25 |
| Algorithm learning speed | Slower | Faster |
| Best for | Awareness → Consideration | Full funnel |
| User intent | Discovery/entertainment | Social/mixed |
| Age skew | 18-34 heavy | 25-54 balanced |
| Minimum viable budget | $3K/mo | $1.5K/mo |

### When TikTok Makes Sense
✅ Your target audience is 18-40
✅ Your product is visual/demonstrable
✅ You can produce native-feeling video content at volume
✅ You have $3K+/month to dedicate to TikTok specifically
✅ You're willing to invest in a creator network for UGC

❌ Skip TikTok if:
- Your audience is 50+ (reach is limited)
- You can't produce 3+ new video creatives per week
- Your budget is <$3K/month (not enough to exit learning phase meaningfully)
- Your product requires long consideration cycles (complex B2B)
