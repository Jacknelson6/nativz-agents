# Content Calendar

When this skill activates, you are building a content calendar. Follow this workflow.

## Phase 1: Strategic Foundation

Confirm or retrieve from memory:
1. **Client/Brand** — Who is this for? Check structured memory for brand guidelines, content pillars, past performance data.
2. **Platforms** — Which platforms? (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn, X)
3. **Posting frequency** — How many posts per platform per week?
4. **Time period** — Weekly, bi-weekly, monthly calendar?
5. **Content pillars** — What 3-5 content themes/categories does this brand use?
6. **Resources** — Video production capacity, graphic design availability, content creator partnerships
7. **Upcoming events** — Product launches, holidays, sales, industry events
8. **Past performance** — What content types/topics performed best? Check memory.

## Phase 2: Content Pillar Development

If content pillars don't exist yet, develop 3-5 pillars:

### Pillar Framework
Each pillar should have:
- **Name** — Short, memorable label
- **Description** — What topics fall under this pillar
- **Goal** — What this pillar achieves (authority, engagement, conversion, awareness)
- **Content ratio** — What % of total content comes from this pillar
- **Formats** — What content formats work best for this pillar

### Example Pillar Distribution
- **Educational (30%)** — Tips, tutorials, how-tos. Builds authority. High saves/shares.
- **Behind the Scenes (20%)** — Process, team, culture. Builds connection. High engagement.
- **Social Proof (20%)** — Testimonials, results, case studies. Builds trust. Drives conversion.
- **Trending/Cultural (15%)** — Trend participation, memes, timely content. Drives reach.
- **Promotional (15%)** — Product features, offers, CTAs. Drives revenue.

## Phase 3: Calendar Building

### Step 1: Map the Time Period
- Mark all holidays, events, product launches, and observances
- Note any platform-specific events (TikTok trends, Instagram feature launches)
- Block dates for any planned campaigns or launches

### Step 2: Assign Content Slots
For each posting slot, assign:
1. **Date and time** (use optimal posting times from account analytics or general guidelines)
2. **Platform** (may differ per slot)
3. **Content pillar** (rotate to maintain balance)
4. **Content type** (video, carousel, static, story, etc.)
5. **Topic/concept** (specific enough to brief a creator or editor)
6. **Hook idea** (the scroll-stopping opening)
7. **CTA** (what action should the viewer take)
8. **Caption direction** (key messaging points)
9. **Hashtag set** (pre-researched)
10. **Status** (idea / scripted / filmed / edited / scheduled / posted)

### Step 3: Ensure Variety
Check that the calendar has:
- Balanced pillar distribution across the period
- Mix of content formats (not all talking head, not all static)
- Variety in hooks and angles (not repetitive)
- Strategic timing (promotional content before sales events, educational content for awareness)
- Platform-appropriate adaptations

| Pillar | Description | Target % | Actual % |
|--------|-------------|----------|----------|

### Week 1: [Date Range]

#### Monday [Date]
| Platform | Time | Pillar | Format | Topic | Hook | CTA | Status |
|----------|------|--------|--------|-------|------|-----|--------|
| Instagram | 10 AM | Educational | Reel | [Topic] | [Hook] | [CTA] | Idea |
| TikTok | 12 PM | Trending | Video | [Topic] | [Hook] | [CTA] | Idea |

...

[Continue for each day of the calendar period]

- [Any production requirements, creator assignments, asset needs]

### Metrics to Track
- [KPIs for this content period]

## Phase 5: Memory Update

1. Store content pillars and posting schedule in working memory
2. Store brand's content preferences and top-performing topics in structured memory
3. Note any seasonal or event-based content that needs advance planning
4. Track which content ideas have been used to avoid repetition
