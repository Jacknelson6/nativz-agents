# Editing Workflow — Expert Knowledge Base

## CapCut Editing Workflow

### Project Setup
1. **Create project** at 1080×1920 (9:16) for social content, 1920×1080 (16:9) for YouTube long-form
2. **Import all footage** into the media library before starting (avoids relinking issues)
3. **Organize clips** into folders: A-roll (main footage), B-roll (cutaways), Graphics, Audio
4. **Set frame rate** to match your footage (30fps standard; 24fps for cinematic; 60fps for slow-mo source)

### Editing Order (Efficient Workflow)
1. **Rough cut** — lay down all A-roll in sequence, trim fat, get the story structure right (~2× final length)
2. **Fine cut** — tighten timing, remove dead space, cut uhms/pauses, nail pacing (~1.2× final length)
3. **B-roll** — layer cutaways, product shots, screen recordings over A-roll to cover jump cuts and add visual interest
4. **Text/graphics** — add text overlays, lower thirds, callouts, transitions
5. **Sound design** — background music, sound effects, audio levels
6. **Color** — color correction first (fix exposure/white balance), then color grade (mood/style)
7. **Final review** — watch at 1× speed on phone (not computer). Check text readability, audio levels, safe zones.
8. **Export** — platform-specific settings (see Export section below)

### CapCut Pro Tips
- **Auto captions** — CapCut's auto-caption is excellent. Generate, then manually fix errors (especially brand names, slang)
- **Keyframe animations** — use for zoom-ins on key moments (subtle 5-10% zoom over 2 seconds adds energy)
- **Speed ramping** — slow-mo (0.5×) for product reveals, speed-up (1.5-2×) for process/montage sections
- **Templates** — save recurring intro/outro sequences as templates for consistency across client content
- **Batch export** — queue multiple projects for overnight export
- **Version control** — duplicate project before major changes. Name: "ClientName_V1", "ClientName_V2_final", "ClientName_V2_final_ACTUAL" (or better: use date-based naming)

## Hook Framework

The hook is the first 1-3 seconds. It determines whether 90%+ of viewers stay or leave. Every piece of content needs a deliberate hook strategy.

### Hook Types

**1. Pattern Interrupt**
Break the expected visual or audio pattern to trigger involuntary attention.
- Hand reaching toward camera
- Object falling/breaking
- Screen going black then flash
- Sudden loud sound
- Unexpected visual (person in weird location, unusual outfit)

**2. Question Hook**
Ask something the viewer feels compelled to answer.
- "Why is nobody talking about this?"
- "What happens when you [unexpected action]?"
- "Did you know [surprising fact]?"
- "Is it just me or…?"

**3. Bold Statement / Hot Take**
Say something provocative or counterintuitive.
- "Stop doing [common practice]. Here's why."
- "[Popular product] is a waste of money."
- "The #1 mistake [target audience] makes"
- "This changed everything for me."

**4. Visual Hook**
The image itself is the hook — no words needed in the first second.
- Dramatic before/after
- Satisfying process (ASMR, cooking, art)
- Beautiful/shocking result shown first
- Fast-motion timelapse

**5. Text-on-Screen Hook**
Bold text overlay that teases the payoff:
- "The $15 product that replaced my $300 routine"
- "Watch until the end to see the result"
- "I can't believe this actually worked"
- "POV: you just discovered [benefit]"

### Hook Metrics
- **Hook rate** (Meta): % of people who watched past 3 seconds. Target: **30%+** for cold audiences, **40%+** for warm.
- **TikTok**: Watch the 1-second and 3-second retention points in analytics. If <50% make it past 3 seconds, your hook is failing.
- **YouTube**: Check the "Intro" retention point (first 30 seconds). If <70% retention at 30 seconds, your hook/intro needs work.

## Pacing & Retention Techniques

### The 3-Second Rule
Something must change every 3 seconds maximum to maintain attention on social platforms:
- Camera angle change
- B-roll cutaway
- Text appearing/changing
- Zoom in/out
- New speaker
- Sound effect
- Scene change

### Pacing by Platform
| Platform | Cuts per minute | Style |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok | 15-25 | Rapid-fire, jump cuts, constant motion |
| IG Reels | 12-20 | Slightly smoother than TikTok, still fast |
| YouTube Shorts | 12-20 | Similar to Reels |
| YouTube Long-form | 6-12 | More breathing room, but still no dead space |

### Retention Techniques
1. **Open loops** — promise something early that you deliver later. "I'll show you the result at the end." Keeps people watching.
2. **Micro-cliffhangers** — at natural pause points, tease the next section: "But here's where it gets interesting…"
3. **Visual variety** — alternate between tight shots, wide shots, B-roll, screen recordings, text slides
4. **Progress indicators** — "Step 1 of 3," numbered lists, progress bars. People stay when they know how much is left.
5. **Pattern interrupt every 30-60s** — for longer content, insert an unexpected element (sound effect, zoom, joke, tangent) to re-capture wandering attention
6. **Remove the boring parts** — ruthlessly. If you're bored watching it back, viewers already left.

## Sound Design & Music Selection

### Music Selection Rules
1. **Match the energy** — upbeat for positive/exciting content, lo-fi for tutorials, dramatic for transformations
2. **Trending audio** — on TikTok and Reels, using trending sounds gives an algorithmic boost. Check TikTok Creative Center for trending sounds.
3. **Volume balance** — music should be 15-25% of voice volume. If you can't clearly understand the speaker, the music is too loud.
4. **Avoid copyrighted music** for any content that might be used as an ad or monetized on YouTube. Use:
   - Platform music libraries (TikTok, IG, YouTube Audio Library)
   - Epidemic Sound ($15/mo, huge library, ad-safe)
   - Artlist ($17/mo, similar quality)
   - CapCut's built-in music library (free with CapCut Pro)
5. **Beat sync** — cut transitions and key moments on musical beats for professional feel. CapCut has auto beat-sync.

### Sound Effects
Strategic sound effects increase engagement by 15-25% (internal testing across social content).
- **Whoosh** — for transitions, text appearing, scene changes
- **Pop/ding** — for bullet points appearing, key moments
- **Bass drop/boom** — for reveals, transformations, dramatic moments
- **Click/snap** — for quick transitions, outfit changes
- **Ambient/room tone** — layer under voiceover for natural feel (prevents "podcast studio" sterile sound)

**Libraries**: Epidemic Sound, Freesound.org, CapCut built-in, YouTube Audio Library

### Audio Levels
| Element | Target Level |
|---|---|
| Dialogue / Voiceover | -6 to -3 dB (peak) |
| Background music | -18 to -24 dB |
| Sound effects | -12 to -6 dB (depends on impact) |
| Overall master | -3 to -1 dB (peak, never clip 0 dB) |

Always check audio with earbuds and phone speakers — most viewers watch on phone.

## Text Overlay Best Practices

### When to Use Text
- **Always** on TikTok and Reels (80%+ of viewers have sound off or low)
- **Captions/subtitles** on all talking-head content across all platforms
- **Key points/callouts** to emphasize important information
- **Hook text** in the first frame

### Text Style Guidelines
- **Font**: Bold, sans-serif (Montserrat, Bebas Neue, Arial Black). Avoid thin/script fonts — unreadable on mobile.
- **Size**: Minimum 40pt equivalent. If you have to squint on your phone, it's too small.
- **Contrast**: White text with black outline/shadow works on any background. Or use a semi-transparent background bar.
- **Position**: Center or upper-third of frame (bottom is covered by platform UI, captions, and CTA buttons)
- **Duration**: Minimum 1.5 seconds on screen (faster = unreadable). 2-3 seconds ideal for a phrase.
- **Animation**: Subtle pop-in or typewriter effect. Don't overdo fancy animations — they distract from the message.
- **Consistency**: Same font, color, and style throughout the video and across a client's content library

### Caption/Subtitle Style
- **Max 2 lines** on screen at once
- **Max 6-8 words per line**
- **Highlight key words** in a different color (CapCut does this well)
- **Center-aligned** or **bottom-third** placement
- **Manual review** — auto-captions are 90% accurate; that 10% can include embarrassing errors

## Color Grading for Social

### Color Correction vs Color Grading
- **Correction** (first): Fix technical issues — white balance, exposure, contrast. Make it look natural.
- **Grading** (second): Apply a creative look — mood, style, brand consistency.

### Quick Correction Workflow
1. **White balance** — use the eyedropper on something white/gray in the frame
2. **Exposure** — adjust so skin tones look natural, highlights aren't blown
3. **Contrast** — slightly increase for punch (social content needs more contrast than film)
4. **Saturation** — bump 5-15% for social (phone screens = saturated viewing environment)

### Social-Specific Grading Tips
- **Warm tones** (slightly orange/golden) perform best for lifestyle, food, beauty content
- **High contrast + slight desaturation** for moody/editorial content
- **Clean and bright** for product content, tutorials, talking heads
- **Consistency across clips** — if shooting with multiple phones/cameras, color match in post
- **Don't over-grade** — social video is consumed fast; subtle grading > dramatic film-look LUTs
- **Apply LUTs at 40-60% intensity** — full-strength LUTs almost always look overdone

### CapCut Color Tools
- **Filters** — quick but limited control. Good for consistency across a series.
- **Adjust panel** — exposure, contrast, saturation, temperature, tint, highlight, shadow
- **HSL** — adjust individual color channels (useful for skin tone correction)
- **Curves** — most powerful tool for precise control (S-curve for contrast, RGB curves for color cast)

## Export Settings Per Platform

### TikTok
- **Resolution**: 1080×1920 (9:16)
- **Frame rate**: 30fps (60fps if source is 60fps)
- **Codec**: H.264
- **Bitrate**: 10-15 Mbps (TikTok compresses heavily; upload at high quality)
- **Audio**: AAC, 128-256 kbps
- **Format**: MP4

### Instagram Reels
- **Resolution**: 1080×1920 (9:16)
- **Frame rate**: 30fps
- **Codec**: H.264
- **Bitrate**: 10-15 Mbps
- **Audio**: AAC, 128 kbps
- **Format**: MP4
- **Note**: Upload via mobile app for best quality (desktop upload compresses more)

### YouTube Shorts
- **Resolution**: 1080×1920 (9:16)
- **Frame rate**: 30 or 60fps
- **Codec**: H.264 (H.265 supported but H.264 is safer)
- **Bitrate**: 15-20 Mbps
- **Audio**: AAC, 256 kbps
- **Format**: MP4
- **Note**: Include #Shorts in title or description (less necessary now but still helps classification)

### YouTube Long-Form
- **Resolution**: 1920×1080 (16:9) minimum; 3840×2160 (4K) if source supports it
- **Frame rate**: Match source (24, 30, or 60fps)
- **Codec**: H.264 (for compatibility) or H.265 (for 4K efficiency)
- **Bitrate**: 20-35 Mbps (1080p), 45-68 Mbps (4K)
- **Audio**: AAC, 256-320 kbps
- **Format**: MP4 or MOV
- **Note**: YouTube re-encodes everything. Upload at maximum quality; let YouTube handle compression.

### General Export Tips
- **Always export at source quality or higher** — platforms compress; you lose quality twice if you compress before upload
- **Check on phone** after export, before upload — does text look readable? Is audio clear?
- **Keep master files** — store original project files and high-quality exports. Clients will ask for re-edits.
- **File naming**: `ClientName_Platform_ContentType_Date_Version.mp4` (e.g., `Nativz_TikTok_ProductDemo_20250223_V2.mp4`)
