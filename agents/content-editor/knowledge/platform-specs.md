# Platform Specs & Best Practices — Expert Knowledge Base

## TikTok

### Technical Specs
- **Aspect ratio**: 9:16 (1080×1920 recommended, 720×1280 minimum)
- **Length**: 1 second to 10 minutes (3 minutes max for most accounts; 10-minute unlock available)
- **File size**: Up to 287.6 MB (iOS), 72 MB (Android), 500 MB (web upload)
- **Formats**: MP4, MOV
- **Frame rate**: 30fps standard, 60fps supported
- **Audio**: AAC, 44.1kHz recommended

### Optimal Content Length
- **Sweet spot**: 15-45 seconds for most content
- **Under 15 seconds**: Works for memes, transitions, punchline content — can loop and rack up views
- **30-60 seconds**: Best for tutorials, storytelling, UGC reviews
- **1-3 minutes**: Works for deep storytelling, "storytime" content, educational content with high retention
- **Over 3 minutes**: Only if retention is genuinely high (rare — algorithm penalizes early drop-off)

### Algorithm Factors (2025)
1. **Completion rate** — #1 factor. Short videos that loop = high completion. Longer videos need extremely strong hooks + pacing.
2. **Rewatch rate** — People watching multiple times signals high quality
3. **Share rate** — Shares (especially to DMs) are the strongest engagement signal
4. **Comments** — Both volume and engagement in comments (replies drive more distribution)
5. **Save rate** — "Saves" signal utility/reference value
6. **Watch time** — Total seconds watched, not just completion
7. **Profile visits after viewing** — Signals the content made viewers curious about you
8. **Sound usage** — Using trending sounds gets a distribution boost

### What Works in 2025
- **Storytelling with payoff** — promise something in the hook, deliver by the end
- **Native, unpolished feel** — phone-quality, authentic settings
- **Text hooks on screen** — 80%+ of top-performing TikToks have text overlay in the first frame
- **Fast-paced editing** — cuts every 2-3 seconds, constant visual change
- **Trending sounds** — even as background music, trending audio boosts initial distribution
- **Series content** — "Part 1, Part 2…" drives follows and returns
- **Reply to comments** as new videos — algorithm loves this engagement loop

## Instagram Reels

### Technical Specs
- **Aspect ratio**: 9:16 (1080×1920)
- **Length**: Up to 90 seconds (from feed), up to 15 minutes (from Reels tab on some accounts)
- **File size**: Up to 650 MB
- **Formats**: MP4, MOV
- **Cover image**: 1080×1920 (separate upload, critical for grid aesthetics)
- **Safe zones**: Keep text/key elements within center 1080×1350 area (top and bottom are covered by UI elements)

### Algorithm Factors
1. **Watch time / completion rate** — same as TikTok, most important signal
2. **Shares** — especially shares to DMs and Stories (strongest signal on IG)
3. **Saves** — IG weights saves heavily for "educational" and "reference" content
4. **Engagement rate** — likes, comments relative to reach
5. **Follows from Reel** — new follows triggered by a Reel = major boost
6. **Audio trends** — using trending audio gives a distribution bump
7. **Original content** — IG deprioritizes content with TikTok watermarks or other platform branding. **Always upload original files, never cross-post with watermarks.**

### Best Practices
- **Hook in first 1-2 seconds** (same as TikTok but IG users scroll slightly slower)
- **Cover image matters** — unlike TikTok, Reels appear on your grid; design cover images that are branded/consistent
- **Carousel Reels** — new format in 2025 combining carousel slides with video; early data shows strong engagement
- **Optimal length**: 15-30 seconds for entertainment, 30-60 seconds for educational
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 relevant hashtags (not 30 — IG's recommendation engine is good enough without hashtag stuffing)
- **Post to Feed + Reels** — always share to main feed for maximum distribution
- **Remix/Collab feature** — co-authoring with another account doubles distribution pools

### IG Reels vs TikTok
| Factor | TikTok | IG Reels |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery reach | Higher for new accounts | Favors existing followers first |
| Content style | Raw, unpolished | Slightly more polished accepted |
| Audience age | 18-34 dominant | 25-44 dominant |
| Best content types | Trends, humor, storytelling | Educational, aspirational, lifestyle |
| Repurposing | Original first, then repurpose | Remove TikTok watermarks |
| Growth speed | Faster (algorithm pushes new creators) | Slower but more stable |

## YouTube Shorts

### Technical Specs
- **Aspect ratio**: 9:16 (1080×1920)
- **Length**: Up to 3 minutes (expanded from 60s in 2024)
- **File size**: Standard YouTube upload limits
- **Format**: MP4, MOV, AVI, WMV, and more
- **Thumbnail**: Auto-selected from video frame (can edit after upload to choose frame)
- **Title**: Up to 100 characters

### Algorithm Factors
1. **View velocity** — how quickly views accumulate in first 1-2 hours
2. **Swipe-away rate** — people swiping past = negative signal (equivalent to TikTok's skip)
3. **Watch time** — total watch time and completion rate
4. **Engagement** — likes, comments, shares
5. **Subscribe rate** — viewers subscribing after watching = strong signal
6. **Channel authority** — unlike TikTok, YouTube factors in your channel's overall performance

### Monetization (2025)
- **YouTube Shorts Fund** has been replaced by **ad revenue sharing**
- Creators earn a share of ad revenue from ads between Shorts in the feed
- Eligibility: YouTube Partner Program (1,000 subscribers + 10M Shorts views in 90 days OR 4,000 watch hours)
- Revenue: Significantly lower than long-form ($0.01-0.07 per 1,000 views vs $2-10 for long-form)
- **Strategy**: Use Shorts to drive subscribers → funnel to long-form for revenue

### Algorithm Differences from TikTok
- YouTube gives **more weight to subscribers** — your Shorts are shown to your subscriber base first
- **Less trend-driven** — evergreen content can resurface weeks/months later on YouTube
- **Thumbnail** doesn't matter as much (Shorts autoplay in feed) but **title** matters more for search
- YouTube Shorts can rank in **YouTube Search** — include keywords in title and description

## YouTube Long-Form

### Technical Specs
- **Aspect ratio**: 16:9 (1920×1080 standard, 4K 3840×2160 for quality)
- **Length**: No maximum (optimal: 8-20 minutes for most content)
- **Thumbnail**: 1280×720, under 2MB, JPG/PNG/GIF
- **Title**: Up to 100 characters (front-load keywords)
- **Description**: Up to 5,000 characters (SEO goldmine)
- **Tags**: Up to 500 characters total (less impactful than they used to be but still add them)

### Thumbnail Best Practices
The thumbnail is **50%+ of whether someone clicks**. Treat it as seriously as the video itself.

Rules:
1. **Faces with emotion** — close-up faces with exaggerated expressions get 30%+ higher CTR
2. **3 elements maximum** — too cluttered = scroll past. One face, one object, one text element.
3. **Bold, readable text** — 3-5 words max. Must be readable on mobile (test at thumbnail size). Use contrast colors.
4. **Bright colors** — yellow, red, orange pop on the YouTube interface (dark background feed)
5. **Curiosity gap** — show enough to intrigue but not enough to satisfy (don't spoil the payoff)
6. **Consistency** — maintain brand style so subscribers recognize your content in their feed
7. **A/B test** — YouTube now supports thumbnail A/B testing natively. Use it.

Target CTR: **4-8%** for established channels, **2-4%** for new channels. Below 2% = thumbnail/title problem.

### Retention Optimization
YouTube ranks videos by **audience retention** — the percentage of the video people watch.

**Retention targets:**
- **50%+ average view duration** = excellent (video will be promoted)
- **40-50%** = good
- **Below 30%** = problem (algorithm will limit distribution)

**Retention techniques:**
- **Cold open** — start with the most interesting/tense moment, not an intro
- **Pattern interrupts every 30-60 seconds** — B-roll cut, zoom, sound effect, graphic, location change
- **Open loops** — tease what's coming later ("but first," "I'll reveal this at the end," "wait until you see #3")
- **Chapters** — break content into clear sections. Viewers who would have left might stay for a specific chapter.
- **Eliminate dead space** — cut uhms, pauses, repetition. Tight editing = higher retention.
- **End screens** — last 20 seconds: CTA to subscribe + link to next video. Don't fade to black.

### Chapters & Cards
**Chapters:**
- Add timestamps in description (first must be 0:00)
- YouTube auto-generates chapter markers on the progress bar
- Improves UX and can appear as rich results in Google Search
- Format: `0:00 Introduction\n1:23 First Topic\n3:45 Second Topic`

**Cards:**
- In-video pop-ups linking to other videos, playlists, or channels
- Place cards at retention drop-off points to redirect viewers rather than losing them
- Max 5 cards per video — don't overdo it

## Platform-Specific Trends (2025)

### Cross-Platform Trends
- **Long-form Shorts/Reels** — 2-3 minute vertical video is the new frontier (all platforms expanded limits)
- **AI-generated content backlash** — audiences increasingly value authenticity; "made with AI" labels are being added and content is being deprioritized
- **Carousel/multi-slide video** — hybrid format gaining traction on IG and TikTok
- **DM-driven engagement** — all platforms now weight DM shares as a top engagement signal
- **SEO in social** — TikTok and IG are becoming search engines; keyword-rich captions matter more than ever
- **Podcast clips** — 30-90 second podcast highlight clips continue to drive growth across all platforms
- **Community features** — all platforms pushing community tabs, broadcast channels, group features

### Platform-Specific
- **TikTok**: Photo carousels with music are having a moment; TikTok Shop integration means shoppable content is prioritized; "TikTok Search" is a real SEO opportunity
- **Instagram**: Broadcast channels for creators; collab posts for dual-audience reach; IG is rewarding original content over reposts more aggressively
- **YouTube**: Podcasts on YouTube growing; community posts drive subscriber engagement; Shorts-to-long-form funnel is the growth strategy
